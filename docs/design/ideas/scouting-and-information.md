# Scouting and Information Ideas

Current observations and merging are defined by [Scouting and Knowledge](../current/scouting-and-knowledge.md). The committed long-term model is described in [Information and Distance](../direction/information-and-distance.md).

## Deeper scouting layers

Flyby observations, deep surveys, uncertainty ranges, or preliminary remnant classifications could sit between current information levels. Hidden truth must remain deterministic and committed by world generation.

Before promotion, define each layer's facts and uncertainty, acquisition actions, merge behavior, TUI distinctions, planning consequences, and deterministic knowledge-transition scenarios. Incomplete scouting must not permanently paywall truth.

## Information decay and contestability

Survey information might age, become contestable, or retain explicit uncertainty beyond the current observation timestamp. A proposal must define which facts can decay, how immutable truth remains protected, and how older and newer evidence merge without randomizing world truth.

## Richer delayed information

Two channels could carry fast thin communications and richer ship-borne observations. Open details include summary and alert payloads, broadcast cadence, delay and merge rules, contradictory ages, freshness presentation, distress calls, remote authority, and interactions with delegation.

A proposal must respect the directional premise that ships are faster than light and can carry information faster than light-based distant communications. It must not describe current reports as carrying stocks, population, infrastructure, or queues when they do not.
