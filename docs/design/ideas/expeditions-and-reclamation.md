# Expedition and Reclamation Ideas

Current bounded founding is defined by
[Ships and Expansion](../current/ships-and-expansion.md). Physical expeditions
and eventual recovery are directional, but the mechanisms below are optional.

## Richer expeditions

Expeditions could carry people, specialists, supplies, equipment, or reclamation
payloads through normal travel. Before promotion, define mission types, loading
and reservation, launch and diversion, deterministic destination outcomes,
loss evidence, scouting constraints, roster granularity (typed counts versus
temporarily individuated members), and authored success/failure/recovery
scenarios.

## Resource ruins

Finite precursor deposits could offer lower-commitment recovery targets. Define
generated truth and evidence, resource kinds and depletion, recovery tools,
physical carriage and loss, possible non-resource discoveries, and deterministic
discovery and retrieval scenarios. Compare discrete strip-and-return missions
with persistent remote worksites rather than assuming either model.

## Site ruins and reclamation

Precursor sites could transition from damaged or ruined developments into
functional infrastructure or outposts rather than forming a parallel building
model. Define states, evidence, body/slot compatibility, people and material
requirements, authority, abandonment, retries, daughter-community outcomes,
whether expedition composition shapes the destination, and deterministic
transition scenarios.

The resource/site distinction is not a committed taxonomy. Both are candidate
ways to realize [pre-existing world texture](../direction/world-structure.md#pre-existing-world-texture).

## Stranded convoys and rescue

A failed convoy might persist as a distress entity instead of disappearing.
Define survival state, observation, rescue windows, cargo and population loss,
and interaction with delayed communication.

## Failure persistence and succession

World-absorbed failure could eventually leave reclaimable community state within
a run or across runs. Origin loss might end a session, transfer the seat to a
daughter community, or become another recoverable event. Persistence boundary,
lineage, succession, and end-state semantics remain deliberately open and must
not be inferred from the directional principle alone.
