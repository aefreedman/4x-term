# Governance and Politics Ideas

## Cultural influence and coherent management

A cultural-influence model could determine where the origin community remains one coherently managed polity. Beyond that reach, remote communities might need delegation, gain local priorities, or gradually develop distinct identities without becoming adversarial factions by default.

Before promotion, define whether influence uses geometric distance, travel time, communication delay, network hops, institutions, or several factors; which commands and information remain direct; how identity changes; and deterministic scenarios for authority transitions. It must refine, not contradict, the [delegation-by-distance direction](../direction/community-governance.md#delegation-by-distance).

## Governor verb set

The boundary between player-exposed policy, AI defaults, remote commands, and presence- or envoy-dependent action remains open. A proposal must define the repeatable tick-to-tick governance verbs and how authority changes with the committed information and delegation direction.

## Environmental conflict

Environmental hazards such as ecological disaster or disease could provide conflict without making conquest the game's focus. Hazards might move along the same physical routes as trade and logistics.

A proposal must define deterministic state and propagation, player-visible evidence, prevention and response verbs, physical consequences, and authored fixtures. Live creation of damaged or ruined places may eventually follow from such hazards, but no environmental conflict or live-ruin mechanic is currently committed.

## Political drift and succession

Remote drift, coalitions, proto-factions, origin succession, and command transfer are open possibilities. They should not be inferred from lore or from the long-term fact that direct micromanagement attenuates with distance.
