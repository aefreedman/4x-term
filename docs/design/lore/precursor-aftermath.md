# Precursor Aftermath

The player inhabits the aftermath of another civilization's maximalist 4X
history. Precursors built megastructures, extraction monocultures, and
stellar-scale Energy grids, then collapsed. The Energy economy is experienced
through the remains of that ruined grid.

The player community is the **origin** community: a fixed home from which
expansion radiates. The thematic loop is a humane, survival-scale form of the
game whose accumulation-driven form produced the wreckage.

Precursor history can inspire generated resource-rich or infrastructure-rich
places, salvage, reclamation, and uneven difficulty. Those examples are not
mechanical facts. Current generation is allowed to contain empty geography and
resources without generated ruins, and no particular ruin taxonomy follows from
this lore.

## Unresolved fiction

The nature of Energy, details of the precursor collapse, the exact age and
condition of remnants, and how much history can be recovered remain open.
Unresolved setting detail may stay on lore pages as long as it is labeled and
does not masquerade as a current mechanic.
