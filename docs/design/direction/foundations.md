# Gameplay Foundations

This is the concise entry point for the design direction that replaced the
authored trader-market prototype. The original mixed G1–G22 document was
decomposed so current mechanics, direction, lore, and ideas no longer share an
ambiguous authority. Its historical labels remain resolvable in the
[legacy mapping](README.md#legacy-g-label-mapping).

## Diagnosis

The earlier slices built economic terrain rather than gameplay. When the
player's verbs are identical to the simulation's accounting verbs, optimal play
collapses into arithmetic. The game instead needs informational, positional,
and governance decisions that use the simulation without merely exposing it.
Long soaks can describe texture but cannot substitute for short deterministic
gameplay scenarios.

## Genre and scope

- The game is a **survival-inflected 4X** about scarcity, coping, and recovery,
  not an uninterrupted power curve toward a win screen.
- It is not anti-expansion, but it is anti-displacement: the frontier is broken
  and sparse rather than a fully populated conquest map.
- Governance is the game. The player begins as the community's governor; there
  is no retained trader-to-governor progression.
- Mechanics lead and fiction develops alongside them. Lore may shape tone and
  terminology but cannot create mechanics by implication.

## Founding direction

The player is the community, generates and allocates physical margin, expands
through physical commitments, and confronts deterministic hidden truth and the
limits imposed by distance. Local collapse is valid world texture rather than a
world-generation failure. Long-term scale comes from governing a network that
cannot be observed or micromanaged instantaneously.

The world begins with one player origin in the aftermath of precursor collapse.
Current generation need not already contain every ruin or recovery mechanic
suggested by that setting. See [Precursor Aftermath](../lore/precursor-aftermath.md)
for canonical context and [World Structure](world-structure.md) for its committed
mechanical direction.
