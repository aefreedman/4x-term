# Community Governance

## The player is the community

The player represents the community rather than an individual with a separate
treasury. Physical stocks and infrastructure belong to systems; population
belongs to the community. Roles may eventually specialize, but the durable goal
is direction-setting rather than per-unit micromanagement.

Current ledgers and direct commandability are defined by
[Systems and Resources](../current/systems-and-resources.md). They remain
authoritative while governance direction evolves.

## Delegation by distance

Long-term control and information are fullest at the origin and attenuate with
distance through the same physical constraints described by
[Information and Distance](information-and-distance.md). Remote communities use
the same policy component under delegated priorities; delegation is not a
pretext for adversarial NPC behavior.

Current founded systems instead become directly commandable after the origin
receives a successful outcome. That is an intentional current-to-direction gap,
not evidence that delegation already exists.

At larger scale the player should preside over a civilization that can be
steered but not instantaneously micromanaged. Political drift, factions,
cultural influence formulas, and exact command boundaries remain
[exploratory](../ideas/governance-and-politics.md).
