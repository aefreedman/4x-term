# Information and Distance

## Deterministic hidden truth

Hidden information is core, but hidden does not mean randomly resolved. World truth is committed by the generated identity. Uncertainty lives in observations and player knowledge while simulation remains deterministic end to end.

## Layered observation

Information arrives in layers rather than switching globally from unknown to omniscient. Current anonymous existence, identified summaries, and complete probe/ship observations are defined in [Scouting and Knowledge](../current/scouting-and-knowledge.md). Additional survey layers may be explored, but truth must not be permanently paywalled and failure must remain attributable to world truth and player commitment.

## Two-channel information

Long-term information uses two complementary channels:

- **Comms** travel at light speed: slower across interstellar distance, thin, and suitable for periodic summaries or alerts.
- **Ships** travel faster than light: physically carried, discrete, and able to deliver richer detail, people, goods, samples, or authority faster than distant light-based communications.

The strategic result is delayed and uneven knowledge rather than instantaneous remote omniscience. World scale and communication behavior must make distance meaningful, and interfaces must show observation freshness.

The exact payloads, cadence, merge rules beyond current observations, authority consequences, and presentation remain [exploratory](../ideas/scouting-and-information.md). This direction does not claim that current reports already transmit stocks, population, queues, or other runtime summaries.
